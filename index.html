Women's pockets, The Pudding

I looked at the story on women’s pockets from The Pudding. My first reaction was that the graphic elements in this story were really aesthetically pleasing without being distracting or too much to look at. The interactivity in the title was a really great idea, and it helps draw the reader in right from the beginning. It’s really creative. Additionally, overall I think the graphics are really simple to understand – even though it’s an alternative and more unusual graphic style, like not a typical chart, it’s still a very intuitive design that readers can easily understand. I also think it’s good that they kept the same style of graphics/dataviz throughout the story. By sticking with the same model, they made it even easier for readers to understand.

My biggest issue with the graphics and dataviz is that the initial two or so graphics have no units or scale. While visually, the graphics and dataviz still allow readers to understand the general idea, when the article is discussing something like a measurement or size, units and scale are critical. I wasn’t able to really get a grasp on the size of the comparison until the third graphic or so.

The most useful graphics to me were the fifth ones where readers could select a commonly-carried object and see how it fit in women’s versus men’s pockets, and could even see the specifics of how it would work for each pair of pants. It gave the most context and was a really interesting comparison. It also made it really easy for me to comprehend the size of the pockets because I had an understanding of the objects listed.
